Category: Core Body Exercises


The core of the body is broadly considered to be the torso.
The core muscles are used for almost every movement of the human body.
So you can easily say that every street workout routine or calisthenics exercise will require a strong core body.
The exercises below are specifically selected to train your core muscles.
The star rating next to each exercise indicates the intensity and difficulty of the workout.

Not only will the core determine for a large part the look of someone’s posture,
research has also shown that persons with higher core stability have a lower risk of injury!

HERE IS A LIST OF CORE BODY EXERCISES TO CONSIDER THE NEXT TIME YOU HIT THE STREETS:

Human Flag

The human flag is an advanced street workout move.
Even for experienced athletes this is one of the more difficult exercises.
The human flag requires a very strong core body.
 Not only does it grab plenty of attention,
but it’s a great full body exercise that trains the side abs, lats, and shoulders.
To achieve this gravity-defying exercise, it’s important to make sure you know how to perform the human flag correctly.
This will help keep you from injuries and ensure you have the best chance to perform this maneuver. 

Planche

Before you start with the first exercise you need some basic strength.
You should be able to do at least 30 push-ups, 20 triceps dips
and hold a plank position for 120 seconds.
Handstand push-ups are also a great way to train your shoulders and prepare for the full planche position. 

Front lever

The front lever is an advanced calisthenics exercise that you will not learn over night.
It requires a lot of training and dedication before you are able to perform a perfect front lever.  

Elbow lever

The elbow lever is a position where the body is held up horizontal to the ground.
But unlike the planche (where there is NO support along the length of the body),
the elbow lever uses one’s elbows as a resting and balancing point for the body.
This makes the skill much easier than a planche.            

Back lever

The back lever exercise is an advanced street workout move and requires a very strong core body.
The exercise is similar to the front lever, but than with your face towards the ground.
It’s a great move to train you arms, shoulders and back. With your body held out horizontally,
your core must support everything to stay in line.
Even experienced athletes will find this a hard move and need some training before they can do a proper back lever.
The key to the back lever is body tension.
Below is explained how you can progress towards a full back lever workout.
When your body tension isn’t strong enough yet, you might want to start with other core body exercises first.

L-sit hang on bar

The L-sit hang is technically not so difficult to perform,
but you will notice that this static position is pretty hard to hold for a few seconds.
The L-sit hang is very effective for your abs and core.
While hanging on the bar you also train your arms and shoulder.
If you prefer similar but more dynamic moves, then have a look at the hanging leg raise or hanging windshield wipers.

Hang on a bar and slowly raise your legs in a 90 degree position as pictured below.

L-sit

The L-sit exercise is a great workout for your abs,
but the rest of your body will benefit from this core body exercise as well.
HOW TO PERFORM THE L-SIT
Keep your legs straight in front of you in a 90 degree position and hold this position as long as possible.
Your full bodyweight should be carried by your arms.
You can either rest on the ground or two parallel bars.
To add more difficulty you can perform an L-sit on your finger tips as shown below.
Alternatively you can do the L-sit while hanging on a bar.
The L-sit hang is easier, because it puts less strain on your chest and shoulder muscles.

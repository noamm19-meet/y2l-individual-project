Category: Upper Body Exercises


An upper body workout is an essential part of every street workout routine.
If you choose the right upper body exercises and execute them properly, you will have no problem getting the strength, tone and shape you want.
The upper body calisthenics below are a great way to strengthen your shoulders, biceps, triceps, chest and other upper body muscles.
Adding variety can spice things up and new impulses will enforce muscle growth. Make sure you always do a proper warming up before you hit the streets!

HERE IS A LIST OF UPPER BODY EXERCISES:

The star rating next to each exercise indicates the intensity and difficulty of the workout.

Switchblade

The switchblade is a dynamic move for advanced athletes.
When you're able to perform many pull-ups and muscle-ups,
it's time to try something new!

Handstand push-up

The handstand push-up – also known as the vertical push-up or press up –
is a push-up exercise where the body is positioned in a handstand.
A proper handstand push-up is performed free-standing, so without wall support.
This advanced move requires significant strength, as well as balance and control of the core body.

Superman Push-uph

The superman push-up requires explosive strength and may be one of the toughest push-up variations to perform.
Start in the regular push-up position, with your hands at shoulder width.
Go down, explode upward and throw your hands out forward,
as though you are Superman flying through the air.
Once your arms are up in the air, pull up your back legs as well,
so that your body is completely airborne and parallel with the ground.
Before you start with the superman push-up you may try the clapping push-up first. 

Clapping Push-ups

Clapping push-ups are a form of plyometric exercise.
This means that the movement is more dynamic and explosive than the regular push-ups which is more controlled.
 Clapping push-ups are great for developing explosive strength in your arms.  
HOW TO DO CLAPPING PUSH-UPS
Start in a regular push-up position with your arms at shoulder width.
Lower yourself to the ground until your chest almost hits the ground.
Push yourself up with explosive strength.
Aim to get yourself as far off of the ground as you can.
Your hands should leave the ground for one or two seconds, so clap your hands before landing.

One arm push-ups

Before you start with one arm push-ups you should be able to do many (at least 30) regular push-ups easily.
The one hand push-up requires strong triceps and shoulders but also balance and a strong core. 
Start in regular push-up position and place on arm on your side.
(putting your hand on your hamstrings will give you a little extra stability and body tension).
You will feel that the wider you spread your legs the easier it becomes to keep your balance.
The challenge here is to keep your legs as close as possible of course.
You could start with a (lighter) inclinde one arm push-up as shown on the right.
Once your shoulders and triceps become stronger,
you could move to a more horizontal position.

T-Push ups

The T-Push up starts like a regular push up,
but you must rotate your body sideways when you push yourself up.
When your arms are fully stretched, your body forms a T-shape.
That’s why it’s called the T-Push up.  
HOW TO DO THE T-PUSH UP EXERCISE
The T-push up is an easy calisthenic exercise to strengthen your shoulder joints and side abs.
To perform the T-Push up:
Start with your hands at shoulder width in the standard push-up position.
Push yourself up and lift one arm from the floor and raise it towards the ceiling whilst twisting your upper body to the side.
Return to the starting position with both hands on the floor.
Repeat this and alternate left and right each time you push yourself up.

Triceps Dips

HOW TO PERFORM TRICEPS DIPS ON A BAR
For triceps dips, find a set of parallel bars, which are often at playground.
Place one hand on each parallel bar.
Straighten your arms and keep your back straight.
Bend your knees if necessary to raise your feet off the ground.
Inhale, bend your elbows behind you and lower your body until your upper arms are parallel with the ground.
Keep your back straight throughout the movement.
Exhale, straighten your arms and return to the starting position. 

Muscle-Ups

Begin in a hanging position with your palms facing away from you and using a false-grip.
Exhale, bend your elbows, keep them close to you and pull your chest up to the bar.
Push down on the bar and raise your hips to the bar as you straighten your arms.
Inhale, slowly reverse the process and return to the starting position. 

Chest Dips

Chest dips are similar to triceps dips, but they target your pectorals instead of your triceps.
Key is that you go lower than with triceps dips.
Chest dips can be performed on a straight bar or parallel bars.
Place your hands on the bar.
Straighten your arms and raise your feet off the ground.
Inhale, bend your elbows out to the sides.
This time, go lower than when your upper arms are parallel with the earth.
Sink until you feel a slight pull across your chest.
You may lean forward slightly at the bottom of the movement.
Exhale, straighten your arms and return to the starting position. 

Decline Push-Ups

With the decline push-up it is important that you position your feet higher than your chest.
This can be done by placing your feet on a park bench, or on a swing for instance.
Place your hands on the ground and slightly wider than your shoulders.
Inhale, bend your elbows and lower your body.
Exhale, straighten your arms and return to the starting position.
The higher you place your feet the greater the challenge.
Ultimately you could progress towards a full handstand push.
